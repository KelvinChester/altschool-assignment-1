Biography

My name is Aguonovo Kenechukwu Kelvin. I am a multi-disciplinary creative and aspiring tech professional from Nigeria with a degree in Electronics Engineering from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka. Over the years, I have developed a wide range of skills across various fields including graphic design, videography, photography, video editing, fashion design, and full-stack web development. My journey into the world of technology and creativity started with a simple desire to express ideas visually and solve everyday problems. While studying engineering, I immersed myself in creative work—designing posters, producing videos, and managing media content for churches and small organizations.

I played key roles in the media teams at Christ Church Chapel UNN and Wesley Cathedral Methodist Church Enugu (Wesley Inspiration TV), where I contributed to video production, livestreaming, and content management. In the course of my journey, I’ve also taken on freelance work, entered graphic design challenges, built YouTube channels, and managed social media content across various platforms.

I enjoy storytelling through visual media and have used these skills in real-world settings. My engineering background also came into play when I built an IoT-based smart power monitoring system as my final year project, combining hardware and software to provide intelligent energy tracking—this experience deepened my interest in backend systems. Although I haven’t yet served in the NYSC or completed my school clearance, I’ve remained committed to self-development despite limited access to resources like a personal laptop. I’ve taken courses in backend development through platforms like ALX and Axia Africa, and I previously interned at Digital Dreams Tech Academy to learn full-stack web development.

Mylong-term vision includes launching two major initiatives: Pishon Creative Academy, a platform that provides tech and creative education while also linking clients with skilled creatives, and TailorTech Africa, a business that merges tailoring with technology to offer smart, automated fitting and customization services. Both ideas reflect my passion for innovation, problem-solving, and entrepreneurship.

I believe that creativity and technology are powerful tools for change. I see myself as someone called to build solutions, empower others, and leave a lasting impact—especially in Africa. Joining the School of Engineering at AltSchool Africa is one of the many steps I’m taking to sharpen my technical skills and become a world-class backend engineer.
